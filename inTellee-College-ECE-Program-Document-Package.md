## 3. Employment Outlook

### 3.1 National Occupational Classification (NOC)

**Occupational group:** Early childhood educators and assistants  

**NOC Code:** 42202  
**NOC TEER:** 2  

*NOC codes are for guidance only; the Ministry of Labour, Immigration, Training and Skills Development (MLITSD) makes the final classification.*

### 3.2 Labour market

The program prepares graduates for employment in licensed child care, early years programs, and similar environments. Regional labour market data is available via government job banks and the college’s career services.

### 3.3 Licenses or certifications

To practise as an Early Childhood Educator in Ontario, **College of Early Childhood Educators (CECE)** registration is required. The inTellee College ECE Diploma satisfies the academic component for CECE registration. No other occupational licenses are needed beyond CECE registration where it applies.

### 3.4 Completion and employment (reference data)

The college monitors completion and employment rates. For up-to-date graduate employment figures, reach out to the program department or Career Services.

### 3.5 Who hires graduates?

Employers include:

- Child care centres  
- Early years centres and EarlyON Child and Family Centres  
- Parent resource centres  
- Pediatric and hospital-based play programs  
- Nursery schools and preschools  
- Kindergarten and school-age programs  
- Special education and inclusive programs  
- Child care resource centres  

### 3.6 Reputation with employers

inTellee College works to establish a solid reputation by matching the ECE program to provincial standards, offering meaningful placements, and developing the competencies that early years employers value.

## 4. Admission & Entrance Requirements

### 4.1 Academic requirement

- **Ontario Secondary School Diploma (OSSD)** or equivalent, **or**  
- **Mature Student** status (applicant must be at least 18 years old)

### 4.2 Supporting documents

Applicants are required to provide:

- Copy of **Canadian Birth Certificate**, **Canadian Passport**, **Canadian Citizenship**, or **Permanent Resident** card  
- Copy of **OSSD** or equivalent (where applicable)  
- **Two (2) letters of professional reference** (each must be signed, dated, and include the referee’s name and phone number)  
- **Application fee** (see the current fee schedule for amount and payment methods)  
- **Proof of SIN** (Social Insurance Number) — *only after acceptance*

### 4.3 Application process

1. Send in a complete application with all requested documents.  
2. The application must include the **non-refundable application fee**.  
3. The college will get in touch to schedule an **interview** and **assessment** (if required).  
4. **English proficiency:** An English proficiency assessment may be required (pass mark as defined by the college).  
5. The admission decision is communicated within about **two (2) weeks** of the interview.  
6. Accepted applicants receive the program schedule and academic calendar.

### 4.4 After acceptance, before placement

Prior to beginning field placement, students must submit:

- **Medical certificate / health form** signed by a physician  
- **Clear Police Vulnerable Sector Check**  
- **Proof of Standard First Aid and Infant/Child CPR (Level C)** certification  

Instructions for obtaining these will be given at enrolment.

### 4.5 Police Vulnerable Sector Check

Ontario requires a clear Police Vulnerable Sector Check for anyone in child care. Applicants complete the designated form and authorize inTellee College to submit it to the correct police service. A clear result must be on file before placement starts. Fees are determined by the local police service.

### 4.6 Medical and CPR

- A medical exam and current immunization (in line with the *Child Care and Early Years Act* and public health) are required before placement.  
- **Standard First Aid** and **Infant/Child CPR – Level C** must be current before placement.

## 7. Program Curriculum & Course Descriptions

### 7.1 Program structure

The ECE Diploma brings together:

- **Academic courses** — theory, curriculum, development, law, ethics, and professional practice  
- **Field placements** — supervised experience with infants, toddlers, preschoolers, and/or school-age children  

Example total program hours: **1,530** (in-class instruction, placement, and enrichment/seminars). The exact breakdown is set by inTellee College and may change; refer to the current program outline.

### 7.2 Dual focus

1. **Practice with children:** Forming responsive, respectful relationships with each child while meeting the needs of the group.  
2. **Partnership with families:** Forming positive, supportive relationships with families and caregivers.

## 8. Placement Guidelines

### 8.1 Placement structure (example)

Each student completes **three (3) in-person placements**, usually six consecutive weeks per placement:

| Placement        | Age range              | Hours (example) |
|------------------|------------------------|------------------|
| Infants          | 3 months–18 months     | 210              |
| Toddlers         | 18 months–30 months    | 210              |
| Preschool/School-age | 2½–5 years or mixed  | 210              |
| **Total**        |                        | **630**          |

### 8.2 Assignment of placements

The Placement Coordinator, in consultation with the program, assigns placement sites. Assignments take into account fit between student and site, availability, and program requirements.

### 8.3 Tutorials and resource visits

Tutorials and resource site visits may be required during placement. They help students prepare for placement and learn about community resources.

### 8.4 Evaluation

- **Academic:** Exams, assignments, and projects.  
- **Placement:** Observation, supervision, and mentoring by placement supervisors and college evaluators. Students are evaluated on set competency areas and any extra assignments.  
- At least one visit by a college supervisor per placement block is standard.

## 9. Professional Recognition & Articulation

### 9.1 College of Early Childhood Educators (CECE)

The inTellee College ECE Diploma is intended to fulfill the academic requirements for **registration with the College of Early Childhood Educators** in Ontario. Graduates who satisfy all CECE conditions may apply to register.

### 9.2 Recognition outside Ontario

Graduates who plan to work in other provinces or jurisdictions should check with the relevant regulatory or licensing body about recognition of the diploma.
